PULIHORA

Pulihora, is a popular, tangy South Indian rice dish from Andhra Pradesh, often prepared for festivals, special occasions, and as temple prasadam. It features cooked rice mixed with a flavorful, spicy tamarind paste, curry leaves, peanuts, and turmeric.

image of pulihora

ingredients

  • rice
  • tamarind pulp
  • tumeruc powder
  • peanuts
  • mustard seeds
  • green and red chillies
  • oil
  • salt

instructions>

  1. Prepare the Rice: Cook rice so the grains are separate, not mushy. Spread it on a wide plate, sprinkle salt, turmeric, and a tablespoon of oil (sesame oil adds the best flavour), and let it cool completely.
  2. Extract Tamarind Pulp: Soak tamarind in hot water for 15 minutes. Squeeze to extract a thick paste and filter out any debris.
  3. Prepare the Tamarind Sauce: Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, chana dal, urad dal, and peanuts. Fry until golden and aromatic.
  4. Add Aromatics: Add dry red chillies, green chillies, curry leaves, ginger (if using), and hing. Fry until the chillies turn crisp.
  5. Cook the Pulp: Pour in the tamarind extract, salt, and jaggery. Simmer on medium heat until the mixture thickens and oil begins to separate from the sides.
  6. Final Mix: Add this thickened tamarind mixture to the cooled rice. Mix gently with your hands or a fork to ensure the rice is evenly coated without breaking the grains.>
